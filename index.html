Lead Testing in Illinois Daycare Facilities

Jason Winik, March 12, 2022.

This data was obtained through a FOIA request from DCFS on August 27th 2021. I am a co-author on a report with EPIC and the Abrams Environmental Law Clinic that raises awareness of the lead problem in daycare facilities and provides policy solutions for DCFS to address the lead problem.
The Lead Testing for Day Care Centers law that was passed in 2017 required all childcare facilities to test for lead if they meet both of the following requirements:
  • The building was constructed on or before January 1st, 2000.
  • Childcare services are provided to children to ages 6 or younger.
All licensed facilities needed to submit proof of testing for lead to the Department of Child and Family Services (DCFS) by May 1, 2019. The figures below show some of the main findings from the analysis to help answer the following questions:
  • How many facilities were required to test but didn't?
  • How many facilities tested above the 2.0 ppb threshold that requires action?
  • Where are the facilities that tested above the threshold?
  • Where do the children exposed to lead live?
