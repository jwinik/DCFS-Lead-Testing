Lead Testing in Illinois Daycare Facilities

Jason Winik, March 12, 2022.

The Lead Testing for Day Care Centers law that was passed in 2017 and ammended in 2018 required all childcare facilities to test for lead if they meet both of the following requirements:
  • The building was constructed on or before January 1st, 2000.
  • Childcare services are provided to children to ages 6 or younger.
All licensed facilities needed to submit proof of testing for lead to the Department of Child and Family Services (DCFS) by May 1, 2019. The figures below show some of the main findings from the analysis and the number of:
  • Facilities that are required to test, but still have not
  • First lead tests results
  • Facilities that tested positive at least once
  • The number of children potentially exposed to lead
